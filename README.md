# From pixels to patterns: high-throughput in-situ imaging unveils soil fauna dynamics over a year in agroforestry systems

### Study questions

By combining time series of faunal activity, root dynamics, and microclimatic data across cropped and treed habitats, we test whether perennial vegetation buffers communities against environmental fluctuations.

We advance general hypotheses:

(i) Land-use effects on soil conditions – Managed systems enhances the variability and extremes of soil conditions, in terms of microclimate, root resource supply and faunal abundance.

(ii) Shift in activity drivers – In cropped soils, faunal activity is strongly coupled to abiotic fluctuations and resource inputs, whereas under trees it is more governed by internal biotic regulation within complex communities.

Together, these hypotheses test the broader idea that structural complexity enhances the self-organization and resilience of soil communities in the face of global change.
